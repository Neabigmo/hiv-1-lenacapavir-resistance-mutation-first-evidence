# Evidence Atlas v1 - Summary

**Created**: 2026-04-21  
**Sources**: 3 key papers (2022-2026)  
**Total entries**: 28 mutation-phenotype associations

## Key Resistance Mutations Identified

### High-level resistance (>50-fold)
- **N57H**: 4,890-fold (PMC12077089)
- **L56V**: 72-fold (PMC12077089)
- **Q67H/N74D**: 150-fold Kd reduction (mBio 2022)

### Moderate resistance (5-50-fold)
- **N74D**: 20-fold Kd, 10-fold EC50 (mBio 2022)
- **Q67H**: 5-fold Kd, 7-fold EC50 (mBio 2022)

### Complete resistance
- **M66I**: Complete resistance to LEN and KFA-012 (mBio 2022)

## Resistance-Associated Mutation (RAM) Positions

Multiple substitutions at key positions:
- **Q67**: H/K/N variants
- **K70**: H/N/R/S variants
- **N74**: D/H/K variants
- **A105**: S/T variants
- **T107**: A/C/N/S variants

## Fitness Cost Observations

- 74% of polymorphic variants (40/54) show impaired infectivity (0.01-77% of WT)
- L56V and N57H: diminished replication capacity in primary T-cells
- Most CA mutants severely impaired in traditional MT-2 assays
- RevLun assay enables phenotyping of low-fitness variants

## Prevalence in Circulating Strains

- Combined LEN RAMs: 0.51% (51/10,057 sequences)
- Q67H: 0.12% (most common)
- L56V: 0.30% (CRF02_AG)
- N57H: 0.47% (subtype D)

## Cross-Resistance

All LEN RAMs remain sensitive to other HIV drug classes (PR, RT, IN inhibitors).
