## Fitness Cost

- **74%** of 54 polymorphic variants show impaired infectivity (0.01-77% WT)
- **M66I**: Nuclear import severely impaired despite enhanced core stability
- **L56V/N57H**: Diminished replication in primary T-cells
- **Most RAMs**: Severely impaired in MT-2 assays, require RevLun assay

## Structural Determinants

### Binding Pocket
- LEN binds hydrophobic pocket at CA hexamer interface
- Conserved CPSF6 binding: Q4, N57, N74, A77, K182
- Conserved CypA binding: G89, P90
- N183: Only non-conserved LEN binding residue (12.31% variability)

### Resistance Mechanisms
- **M66I**: β-branched Ile66 creates steric clash with LEN R3/R4 rings
- **N57H**: Loss of hydrogen bonds
- **L56V**: Steric clash with di-fluoro-benzyl group
- **Selective**: RAMs preserve CPSF6/NUP153/SEC24C binding

## Pharmacokinetics
- **Half-life**: 10-12 weeks (subcutaneous)
- **Clearance**: Intestinal excretion (major pathway)
- **Formulation**: Long-acting injectable

## Cross-Resistance
- **None** with other ARV classes (PR, RT, IN inhibitors)
- **None** with entry inhibitors

## Key Insights

1. **Genetic barrier**: Low natural prevalence (<1%) supports broad efficacy
2. **Fitness constraint**: Resistance mutations carry substantial replication cost
3. **Subtype variation**: Minimal impact on susceptibility across clades
4. **Clinical context**: Emergence linked to functional monotherapy
5. **Structural selectivity**: Mutations target LEN pocket without disrupting host factor binding
